SITUATION

In the heart of French wine country yet within each reach of major cities, coastal resorts and winter sports venues

A charming village house in the centre of the favoured Malpère triangle: Carcassonne - Limoux - Mirepoix; an area steeped in both 12th century Cathar history and modern winemaking. 

Situated in the foothills of the Pyrenees with easy access to Carcassonne, Limoux, Mirepoix, Revel and Canal du Midi, and about one and a half hours from the coast. Spain a little further afield - a journey of about two and a half hours.

The village of Mazerolles is a traditional circulade* - a circular medieval development planned around the church and chateau for defence. It has the added benefit of a thriving school, daily bread deliveries and épiceries ambulantes for meat, fish and basics. 

It is a pleasant walk through the vineyards to Belvèze for further facilities - supermarket, restaurant, butcher, doctors, chiropodist, physiotherapist 

This compact property offers a great opportunity to live life at an affordable price being immediately habitable whilst allowing scope for your own stamp.

About the property 

This compact property offers a great opportunity to live life at an affordable price being immediately habitable whilst allowing scope for your own stamp.

The front door opens into a living area with an open fireplace.

Being originally two properties, the connecting area bears witness to the circular nature of the original village plan and contains a sink, cupboards and space for refrigerator

Next lies the dining room with salle d’eau (toilet and shower) and staircase leading to a two large double beds.

A useful space off the dining area - believed to be the former cobbler’s workshop - is currently in use for cooking facilities and also has plumbing for a washing machine. 

The stairs lead to two bedrooms. The larger (26sq m) also has a large walk-in storage space whilst the other bedroom displays beautiful original colombage and is semi-divided to form a double bedroom and an additional single bed area. Both bedrooms benefit from wash basins. 
In short, your own pied-à-terre set in a traditional wine producing area with acres of vines and sunflowers surrounding.

There is no outside space;  the house is situated on a quiet alley- Rue du Chardonnay - too narrow for cars to pass the property - and immediately outside this property is a beautiful view of the Pyrenees .

Ideal for holidays and weekends away or longer if you wish. If you are searching for an easy to maintain hideaway, this may be the answer. 

*In the Languedoc-Roussillon region of the south of France, a circulade is a traditional village that has been built in concentric circles. The center, which one might expect to be the site of a château-fort or a parish church
or both!
